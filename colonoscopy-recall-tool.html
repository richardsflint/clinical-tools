None No significant family history
Category 1 Slightly above-average risk
One first-degree relative (FDR) with CRC diagnosed aged 55 years or older.
Category 2 Moderately increased risk
Either of:
  • One FDR with CRC diagnosed younger than 55 years, OR
  • Two FDRs on the same side of the family with CRC at any age (no Category 3 features)
Category 3 Potentially high risk
Any one of the following:
  • Two FDRs, or one FDR + one or more SDRs (same side), with CRC and one relative: diagnosed ≤54 yrs, multiple CRCs, or Lynch-associated tumour (endometrial, ovarian, stomach, small bowel, renal pelvis, pancreas, brain)
  • Known family history of FAP, Lynch syndrome, or other familial CRC syndrome
  • One FDR + two or more first/second-degree relatives (same side) with CRC at any age
  • First- or second-degree relative with CRC and multiple adenomas
  • FDR with ≥10 adenomas by age 30

Source: Canterbury Community Health Pathways – Colorectal Cancer Risk
